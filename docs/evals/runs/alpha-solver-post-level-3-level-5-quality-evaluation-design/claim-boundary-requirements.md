# Claim Boundary Requirements

## Current claim boundary

This packet supports only the claim that a docs-only Level 5 quality evaluation design packet has been created. It does not establish quality evidence or readiness evidence.

## Future quality claims

A future execution lane may make a bounded quality evidence statement only if the statement names the task set, model/provider boundary, scoring rubric, reviewer coverage, score results, limitations, and invalidated tasks. Broad claims such as overall model quality, general product quality, or user-facing readiness remain blocked unless later lanes explicitly authorize and support them.

## Benchmark claims

Benchmark claims require a later authorized benchmark execution lane with frozen benchmark definition, task manifest, raw outputs, score records, environment metadata, and interpretation limits. This packet does not run benchmarks and does not authorize benchmark marketing claims.

## Superiority claims

Alpha superiority claims require later comparative evaluation design and execution with matched tasks, matched conditions, preserved outputs, blinded or controlled review where appropriate, and predeclared statistical or qualitative interpretation criteria. No Alpha superiority claim is authorized here.

## Readiness claims

MVP readiness, product readiness, production readiness, dashboard readiness, `/v1/solve` readiness, provider fallback readiness, hosted fallback readiness, and billing readiness remain blocked. Quality evaluation design is a prerequisite for later product-surface design, but it is not sufficient to prove any readiness claim.

## Local model quality claims

Local model quality claims remain blocked unless a later lane explicitly authorizes local model inference, preserves local environment metadata, records raw outputs, scores outputs under a frozen rubric, and states exact local model/version boundaries.

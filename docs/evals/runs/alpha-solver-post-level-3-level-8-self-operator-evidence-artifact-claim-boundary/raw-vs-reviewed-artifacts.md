# Raw vs Reviewed Artifacts

## Raw artifacts

Raw artifacts are unpromoted records such as logs, transcripts, screenshots, notes, forms, or generated files. Raw artifacts may show what was captured, but they do not prove readiness or capability.

Raw artifacts can support only narrow preservation claims, including path, timestamp if present, source lane if present, and captured content.

## Reviewed artifacts

Reviewed artifacts have been examined by a defined reviewer or review lane. Review can improve artifact trustworthiness for the reviewed scope, but review does not automatically promote evidence beyond that scope.

Reviewed artifacts can support only the specific review outcome recorded in the artifact or packet.

## Boundary rule

Raw and reviewed artifacts both remain blocked from proving autonomous operation, production readiness, provider readiness, model quality, benchmark performance, deployment readiness, browser automation readiness, billing readiness, or external-action readiness unless a later approved promotion lane states otherwise.

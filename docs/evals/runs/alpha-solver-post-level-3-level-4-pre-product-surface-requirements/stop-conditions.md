# Stop Conditions

A later lane must stop and move to fallback when any condition below appears.

## Evidence stop conditions

- Required source evidence is missing.
- Evidence is stale for the claim being considered.
- Evidence contradicts a prior accepted decision.
- Evidence cannot be attributed to an artifact path.
- Evidence is generated by a command log where an authoritative decision file is required.
- Evidence is overbroad relative to the lane.
- Evidence would promote Level 2 or Level 3 beyond the accepted boundary.

## Safety stop conditions

- The lane would expose or call `/v1/solve` without separate authorization.
- The lane would expose or call a dashboard without separate authorization.
- The lane would call hosted providers without separate authorization.
- The lane would add provider fallback or hosted fallback without separate authorization.
- The lane would run local model inference, Ollama, smoke validation, or benchmarks without separate authorization.
- The lane would perform billing work without separate authorization.
- The lane would update Google Sheets or backlog workbooks.

## Documentation stop conditions

- Blocked claims are absent or ambiguous.
- Selected next lane state is contradictory.
- The packet selects more than one next lane.
- A design-only packet is treated as execution evidence.
- Product surface design, provider orchestration design, dashboard planning, API planning, billing planning, or MVP readiness review begins before the required gates are accepted.

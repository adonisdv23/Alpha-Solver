# Blocked Claims

The following blocked claims must not appear in future product-surface UI copy, API response copy, dashboard copy, release copy, sales copy, operator copy, or documentation unless a later explicitly authorized lane creates bounded evidence for the exact claim and preserves the evidence boundary.

## Quality and benchmark claims

Blocked claims include:

- Alpha Solver quality is validated.
- Alpha Solver is accurate, reliable, high-quality, or robust.
- Alpha Solver has benchmark evidence.
- Alpha Solver passed a benchmark.
- Alpha Solver is benchmark-ready.
- Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, Level 5, or this packet proves output quality.

## Superiority claims

Blocked claims include:

- Alpha Solver is superior to plain prompting.
- Alpha Solver outperforms other solvers, agents, models, products, or workflows.
- Alpha Solver is best-in-class.
- Alpha Solver beats a baseline without a separately accepted comparative methodology.

## Readiness and launch claims

Blocked claims include:

- Alpha Solver is MVP-ready.
- Alpha Solver is production-ready.
- Alpha Solver is release-ready.
- Alpha Solver is customer-ready.
- Alpha Solver is safe for general use.
- Alpha Solver is approved for public product use.

## Surface, provider, billing, and fallback claims

Blocked claims include:

- The dashboard is ready, validated, or safe to expose.
- `/v1/solve` is ready, validated, or safe to expose.
- Hosted providers are ready, validated, or safe to use.
- Provider orchestration is ready, validated, or safe to use.
- Billing is ready, validated, or safe to use.
- Provider fallback or hosted fallback is available, validated, or safe to use.

## Evidence-promotion claims

Blocked claims include:

- Local evidence may be promoted to public claims.
- Level 2 or Level 3 evidence authorizes product claims.
- This safety and claim gates packet authorizes product claims.
- Docs-only design evidence is equivalent to execution evidence.

# Non-Promotion Rules

## Core non-promotion rule

Evidence may not be promoted beyond its accepted boundary. Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, Level 5, and this packet must remain bounded to the purpose, method, artifacts, and decisions recorded for each packet.

## Prohibited promotions

Future work must not promote:

- local operator-usability evidence into quality evidence;
- local orchestration evidence into benchmark evidence;
- docs-only design into execution evidence;
- acceptance of a packet into product readiness;
- internal guardrail text into user-facing claim authority;
- bounded observations into Alpha superiority;
- static documentation checks into runtime readiness;
- absence of known blockers into MVP, production, dashboard, `/v1/solve`, provider, billing, fallback, or readiness claims.

## Required non-promotion phrasing

When citing this packet, future work must state that it is a docs-only safety and claim-gate design artifact and does not authorize claims, implement UI/API copy, expose `/v1/solve`, expose dashboards, run models, run benchmarks, call providers, perform billing work, or promote evidence.

## Level 6 usage control

Level 6 controls whether and how this packet is used. Level 6 may adopt, revise, supersede, or reject these gates for a specific future lane, but this packet alone cannot promote evidence.

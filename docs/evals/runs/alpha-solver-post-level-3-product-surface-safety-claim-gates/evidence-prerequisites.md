# Evidence Prerequisites

Before any future product-surface claim is considered, the future lane must provide claim-specific evidence that satisfies all prerequisites below.

## Artifact prerequisites

Required evidence must be:

- produced by an explicitly authorized lane;
- recorded in repository artifacts or other approved evidence records;
- attributable to exact commands, dates, environments, sample sets, and acceptance gates;
- bounded to the exact claim under consideration;
- current enough for the surface being considered;
- reviewed for contradictions against accepted Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, Level 5, and post-Level-3 boundaries.

## Claim prerequisites

A future claim may be considered only when the evidence identifies:

- the exact claim text;
- the exact audience and surface where the claim would appear;
- the exact user action the claim could influence;
- the artifacts supporting the claim;
- the limitations and non-generalization boundary;
- the blocked claims that still remain blocked.

## Required negative evidence check

A future lane must also show that the proposed claim does not imply any blocked quality, benchmark, superiority, MVP, production, dashboard, `/v1/solve`, provider, billing, fallback, or readiness claim.

## Insufficient evidence

The following are insufficient by themselves:

- docs-only packets;
- command logs without authoritative decision files;
- local operator-usability evidence;
- non-promotional local orchestration evidence;
- design packets;
- historical acceptance markers;
- screenshots without acceptance criteria;
- sample outputs without scorer or review methodology.

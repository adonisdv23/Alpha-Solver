# Blocked Provider Claims

Lane:
`ALPHA-SOLVER-POST-LEVEL-3-PROVIDER-SAFETY-CLAIM-GATES-PACKET-001`

## Blocked claims

The following provider claims are blocked by this packet:

- **Provider readiness**: no claim that any local, hosted, or third-party provider is configured, usable, stable, reliable, supported, ready, integrated, or production-capable.
- **Hosted readiness**: no claim that hosted models, hosted inference, hosted credentials, hosted rate limits, hosted observability, hosted fallback, hosted billing, or hosted support are ready.
- **Fallback readiness**: no claim that fallback exists, is safe, is automatic, is tested, preserves determinism, preserves cost controls, or provides continuity.
- **Billing readiness**: no claim that provider costs, billing, metering, budget enforcement, invoice reconciliation, or customer billing are ready.
- **Production readiness**: no claim that provider-backed Alpha Solver behavior is production-ready, deployable, operationally supported, generally available, or customer-ready.
- **MVP readiness**: no claim that provider-backed Alpha Solver behavior is MVP-ready, launch-ready, demo-ready, pilot-ready, or route-ready.
- **Benchmark claims**: no claim about provider-backed benchmark scores, latency, throughput, pass rate, accuracy, reliability, cost-performance, or ranking.
- **Model-quality claims**: no claim that any provider-backed model is high quality, best, reliable, accurate, safe, robust, deterministic, or adequate for production use.
- **Alpha superiority claims**: no claim that Alpha Solver is better than another solver, better than a provider baseline, superior to a benchmark, or uniquely capable because of provider orchestration.
- **API readiness**: no claim that `/v1/solve` or any provider-backed route is exposed, stable, compatible, authenticated, monitored, rate-limited, or safe.
- **Dashboard readiness**: no claim that provider status, cost, fallback, quality, billing, or operational dashboards are exposed or ready.
- **Evidence promotion**: no claim that Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, Level 5, Level 6, or this packet promotes local evidence into provider-backed evidence.

## Claim default

If a provider-backed claim is not explicitly supported by current, concrete, accepted evidence, it is blocked. Unsupported readiness wording must be removed or rewritten as bounded docs-only design wording.
